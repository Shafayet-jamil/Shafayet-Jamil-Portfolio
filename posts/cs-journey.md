# What I've Learned in My First Year of CS

A year ago I started my Computer Science degree with two things: a laptop and the vague idea that I liked "computers."

Now, a year in, here's what I actually learned — not just technically, but about the whole experience.

## The Hard Parts Nobody Warns You About

### 1. It's less typing and more thinking

I thought CS was mostly about writing code. It's actually mostly about thinking through problems. Code is just how you express the solution.

The hardest part of any assignment isn't syntax — it's figuring out *what* to write before you write it.

### 2. You will be confused a lot. That's fine.

Everyone in class looked like they understood everything. Turns out, most of them didn't either. We were all just quietly confused together.

Confusion isn't a sign that you're bad at this. It's a sign that you're learning something new.

### 3. The basics matter more than you think

I rushed through basic stuff to get to the "cool" things. Bad idea.

Not understanding how loops really work, or what a function actually does, comes back to bite you later. Slow down on the fundamentals.

## What Surprised Me

**How connected everything is.** Math connects to algorithms. Algorithms connect to code. Code connects to systems. Once you start seeing the connections, it's genuinely exciting.

**How much you learn by building things.** Lectures are great. But the moments I learned the most were when I sat down and actually built something — even if it was small and bad.

**Stack Overflow is not cheating.** Professional developers use it every day. The skill is knowing how to search for what you need and understanding the answer you find.

## What I'd Tell Myself on Day One

1. **Don't compare your progress to others.** Everyone starts from a different place.
2. **Build small projects constantly.** Don't just consume courses — create things.
3. **Write about what you learn.** Explaining forces you to actually understand.
4. **Ask for help sooner.** Struggling for 3 hours on something someone could explain in 5 minutes is not productive.
5. **It's supposed to feel hard.** If it felt easy, you wouldn't be growing.

---

I'm still very much a beginner. But I'm a much better beginner than I was a year ago, and that feels good.

Onwards to year two.
